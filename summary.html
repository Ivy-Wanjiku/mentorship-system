Summary of Born a Crime

Chapters one and two it is a summary of the Trevor Noah takes up his life of growing up in South Africa during the apartheid rule, One of the themes that emerges in this chapter is the concept of race which is impactful in his life. As a mixed-race child, Trevor was considered as coloured during the apartheid system. Which classified people based on the apartheid system. This book clarifies the challenges he faced growing up in society. That is strictly enforced by racial segregation and discrimination. Another theme that is evident in this book is the power of language and communication, Trevour discusses the various languages spoken in South Africa. Including South Africa, Africannas, Zulu, Xhosa. Trevor's ability to speak various languages assisted him to adapt and connect with different types of people. Despite the restrictions.In the first two chapters of "Born a Crime" by Trevor Noah, there are several themes that are evident. One of the themes is racism and apartheid. Trevor Noah, being born to a black mother and a white father during the apartheid era in South Africa, shares his experiences of navigating the complexities of racial segregation and discrimination. He highlights the absurdity and injustice of apartheid laws and how they affected his life.Another theme is identity and belonging. Trevor Noah explores his mixed-race heritage and the challenges he faced in a society that classified him as "colored" and forced him to fit into specific racial categories. He reflects on his struggles to find his place and identity in a divided society and the importance of embracing one's true self.
