Summary of Born a Crime

Chapter 1 provides an overview of the historical context in South Africa before and during apartheid,focusing on the rivalry between the Zulu and Xhosa tribes. It highlights the impact of apartheid on the country's people, with particular emphasis on the experiences of Nelson Mandela, who was Xhosa like Trevor Noah's mother. Trevor Noah, the author, introduces himself as a product of apartheid, born in 1984, and presents anecdotes from his childhood that illustrate the complexities of living in a racially segregated society. The chapter delves into Trevor's upbringing within a devout Christian family, where attending church was a significant part of their weekly routine. Despite the logistical challenges of getting to various churches, Trevor's mother's unwavering faith in God is highlighted, along with an incident where she bravely confronts a menacing bus driver, ultimately saving Trevor's life.

Chapter 2 deals into the apartheid regime, tracing its origins back to Dutch colonizers in the 17th century and its formalization by the Afrikaners after British rule ended. Trevor shares the story of his birth, being born to a Black mother and a white Swiss father, which was illegal under apartheid laws. His mother's courage in navigating the complexities of interracial relationships, including seeking sanctuary with a Swiss friend in Johannesburg to give birth, is portrayed.
In Kiambu County, children born to white fathers and black mothers are often discriminated against and bullied because of their skin color..This issues has become a significant challenge for families.
